# Voice, Hooks, and Social Best Practices

## Voice

PsiAN should sound:
- professional
- measured
- educational
- grounded in evidence
- clear without being dramatic

PsiAN should not sound:
- flashy
- trendy for the sake of being trendy
- alarmist
- preachy
- overly casual

## The Hook Formula

Good PsiAN hooks usually do one of these:
- name a problem people recognize
- challenge a common assumption
- lead with a statistic or concrete fact
- point to a gap between what people are told and what PsiAN believes
- highlight a policy, insurance, or privacy issue in plain language

## Hook Examples

- "Most people want therapy that gets to the root of the problem. PsiAN has research on that."
- "Not every 'evidence-based' pitch tells the full story."
- "When therapy becomes a product, patients notice."
- "Insurance problems are not just admin problems. They affect care."
- "AI can assist with language. It cannot replace a therapeutic relationship."

## Post Structure

Use a simple structure:
1. Hook
2. One or two supporting points
3. Why it matters
4. A PsiAN-specific ending

Example shape:
- line 1: hook
- line 2 to 4: explanation
- final line: call to action, site mention, or PsiAN position

## Platform Notes

### Instagram / Facebook / Threads
- Lead with the strongest line
- Keep paragraphs short
- Use readable spacing
- End with a clean link or action

### LinkedIn
- Slightly more formal
- More context is okay
- Keep the argument structured
- Avoid sounding like corporate PR

### X / Twitter
- Make the first post a very clear thesis
- Use replies for the explanation
- Keep each tweet tight
- Do not overload with hashtags

### Bluesky
- Short, direct, and readable
- Often works best as a compact summary

## Best Practices

- Use one main idea per post
- Make the first sentence do real work
- Pull language from PsiAN's site instead of inventing a generic nonprofit voice
- If a page or article has a strong phrase, turn that into the hook
- Use concrete language over abstract wording
- Write for clarity first, then polish

## What Makes A Better Hook

Better:
- specific
- grounded
- short enough to scan

Worse:
- generic
- vague
- overexplained

## Common Mistakes To Avoid

- starting with background filler
- using too many concepts in one post
- writing one long paragraph and hoping it reads well on mobile
- repeating the same sentence in different words
- sounding like a newsletter instead of a social post
